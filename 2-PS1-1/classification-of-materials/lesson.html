Classification of Materials

What is Classification?

When we classify things, we put them into groups based on shared qualities or properties. Scientists and engineers classify materials to better understand them and decide how to use them.

Classify: To group objects based on shared qualities or properties.

Material: Any solids, liquids or gas that can be used to make things.

Property: A quality or characteristic of something.

Classification by Color

One of the easiest ways to classify materials is by their color. When we classify by color, we put all objects with the same color into one group.

Objects sorted into groups based on their colors

In the image above, you can see how objects can be sorted by color. This is one of the simplest ways to classify materials. Notice how all the red items are grouped together, all the blue items together, and so on.

Classification by Texture

Texture describes how something feels when you touch it. Is it smooth or rough?

Different materials showing a variety of textures

Texture: How rough or smooth something is.

Smooth: Having a very flat surface, such as glass.

Rough: Having an uneven surface, such as sandpaper.

Examples of rough and smooth surfaces

Smooth materials: glass, plastic, polished wood, silk

Rough materials: sandpaper, brick, tree bark, unpolished stone

Looking at the images above, you can see examples of different textures. Notice how some surfaces appear bumpy or uneven (rough), while others look flat and even (smooth).

Texture is very important when designing things. For example, a slide needs to be smooth to make it easy and fun to slide down. Can you imagine trying to slide down a rough, bumpy slide? Ouch!

A smooth slide allows for safe and fun sliding

Classification by Hardness

We can also classify materials by how hard or soft they are.

Examples of hard and soft materials

Hard materials: wood, metal, stone, ceramic, glass

Soft materials: cotton, foam, pillows, blankets, clay

Looking at the image above, you can see examples of hard and soft materials. Hard materials keep their shape and usually feel solid when you press on them. Soft materials might change shape when you press on them.

Hardness is very important when choosing materials for specific purposes. Building materials need to be hard in order to build strong and sturdy houses or buildings.

Construction requires hard, strong materials for durability

Diamond is one of the hardest natural materials on Earth. It's so hard that it can scratch almost any other material!

Choosing the Right Materials

When we make things, we need to choose materials with the right properties. Here are some examples:

Slide: Needs to be smooth so people can slide down easily (as shown in the slide image above).

Hammer: Needs to be hard so it can push nails into wood.

Helmet: Needs to be hard to protect your head.

Winter coat: Needs to be soft and warm to keep you comfortable.

Building materials: Need to be hard and strong to support weight (as shown in the construction image).

Why would it not be a good idea to make a hammer out of clay?

Clay isn't hard enough to push a nail through wood. That's why hammers are made of strong materials like metal.

How Temperature Affects Materials

Did you know that temperature can change the properties of materials?

Ice cream: Hard when frozen, soft and melty when warm

Chocolate: Hard and solid when cool, soft and melty when warm

Water: Liquid at room temperature, solid (ice) when very cold, gas (steam) when very hot

Some special metals can "remember" their shape. If you bend them and then heat them up, they'll return to their original shape!

Classifying by Multiple Properties

Sometimes we need to consider more than one property when classifying materials.

For a water slide, we need a material that is:

  • Smooth (for easy sliding, like the slide in the image)
  • Hard enough to be sturdy and support weight
  • Waterproof

For a building (as shown in the construction image), we need materials that are:

  • Hard and strong to support weight
  • Durable to last a long time
  • Weather-resistant

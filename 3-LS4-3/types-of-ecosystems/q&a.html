1. What is an ecosystem? Who coined this term and when?
An ecosystem is a community of life forms (living components) interacting with non-living components in their environment. The term was coined by English scientist A.J. Tansley in 1953. Think of it as nature's interconnected web where everything plays a crucial role! üï∏Ô∏è
2. What makes up an ecosystem? Explain with examples.
An ecosystem consists of:
1. Living components (biotic factors): - Plants - Animals - Microorganisms
2. Non-living components (abiotic factors): - Sunlight - Water - Soil - Air
Example: In a garden ecosystem, plants (living) depend on sunlight, water, and soil (non-living) to grow and survive. üå±
3. What are the two main types of ecosystems? How small or large can an ecosystem be?
The two main types are:
1. Terrestrial ecosystems (land-based)
2. Aquatic ecosystems (water-based)

Size of ecosystems: - Can be as small as a pond - Can be as large as an ocean - Everything in our surroundings is part of some ecosystem! üåç
4. Describe the forest ecosystem in detail.
Forest ecosystems are characterized by:
1. Vegetation: - Many varieties of trees - Different types of plants - Various herbs
2. Animal life: - Birds - Reptiles - Insects - Large mammals
3. Rich biodiversity with complex interactions between species
4. Abundant plant and animal life due to favorable conditions üå≥
5. How do grassland ecosystems differ from forest ecosystems?
Key differences:
1. Vegetation type: - Grasslands: Dominated by grasses - Forests: Dominated by trees
2. Tree density: - Grasslands: Few or no trees - Forests: Abundant trees
3. Both ecosystems support different types of animals adapted to their specific environments ü¶í
6. What are the unique characteristics of a desert ecosystem?
Desert ecosystems are characterized by:
1. Climate: - Hot and dry conditions - Scanty rainfall
2. Landscape: - Mostly barren land
3. Adaptations: - Plants like cactus that can store water - Animals like camels that can survive with little water
4. Limited biodiversity compared to forests üåµ
7. What makes the tundra ecosystem unique?
Tundra ecosystems are unique because:
1. Climate: - Extremely cold conditions - Covered with ice most of the time
2. Vegetation: - Limited variety of plants - No tall trees
3. Special adaptations: - Plants and animals have specific adaptations to survive extreme cold
4. Located in cold climatic regions ‚ùÑÔ∏è
8. How are aquatic ecosystems classified? What are their key differences?
Aquatic ecosystems are classified based on salt concentration:
1. Marine Ecosystem: - Higher salt concentration - Includes seas and oceans - Wide variety of organisms - Larger in size
2. Freshwater Ecosystem: - Lower salt concentration - Includes rivers, ponds, lakes, streams - Comparatively fewer varieties of organisms - Generally smaller in size üåä
9. Why is the interaction between living and non-living components important in an ecosystem?
Interactions are crucial because:
1. Survival depends on these interactions - Plants need sunlight, water, and soil to grow - Animals need plants and other animals for food
2. Creates balance in nature
3. Maintains the ecosystem's health
4. Ensures continuation of life processes üîÑ
10. How do organisms adapt to different ecosystems?
Organisms adapt differently in each ecosystem:
1. Desert adaptations: - Cacti store water - Camels can survive with little water
2. Tundra adaptations: - Animals have thick fur - Plants grow close to ground
3. Aquatic adaptations: - Fish have gills - Marine plants can survive in salty water
These adaptations help organisms survive in their specific environments! ü¶é

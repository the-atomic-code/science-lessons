Basic Energy Concepts

Q1: What is energy in its classical definition?
Energy is the ability to do work.
Q2: What is the law of conservation of energy?
Energy can neither be created nor destroyed. It can only be transformed from one form to another. The total energy in an isolated system (like the universe) remains constant.

Types of Energy Sources

Q3: What are the two main classifications of energy sources?
Energy sources are classified into:
  • Renewable sources
  • Non-renewable sources
Q4: What defines renewable energy sources?
Renewable energy sources:
  • Are natural resources that cause minimal environmental impact
  • Are available in abundance in nature
  • Can be naturally refilled
  • Are sustainable and environmentally safe
Q5: List all the renewable energy sources mentioned in the video.
  • Solar energy
  • Wind energy
  • Biomass
  • Geothermal energy
  • Hydropower energy
Q6: How is solar energy utilized?
Solar energy can be used in two main ways:
  • Converted into electrical energy using solar panels
  • Converted into thermal energy for heating homes and water
Q7: How is wind energy harnessed?
Wind energy is harnessed using wind turbines, which convert wind power into electricity that can power homes and schools.
Q8: What is biomass and what are its sources?
Biomass is renewable organic matter that comes from:
  • Plants
  • Animals
  • Wood
  • Crops
  • Animal waste
These materials can be converted into fuel.
Q9: What is geothermal energy and how is it used?
Geothermal energy comes from the heat inside the Earth. It can be used to:
  • Produce electricity
  • Heat homes and buildings
  • Provide hot water
Q10: What are non-renewable energy sources and their examples?
Non-renewable sources are those that can run out of supply over time. Examples include:
  • Natural gas
  • Coal
  • Petroleum
  • Nuclear energy
  • Hydrocarbon gas liquids
Q11: Why should we limit our dependency on non-renewable energy sources?
We should limit non-renewable energy use because:
  • They can be exhausted
  • They cause environmental pollution
  • They contribute to carbon dioxide emissions
  • They are not sustainable for the future
Q12: What is hydropower and what makes it special?
Hydropower:
  • Is energy generated from moving water
  • Is one of the oldest renewable energy sources
  • Is one of the largest sources of renewable energy
  • Is abundantly available on Earth

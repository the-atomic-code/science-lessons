Amazing Animal Parts: How Animals Use Their External Parts to Survive

What Are External Animal Parts?

External parts of an animal are any parts found on the outside of its body. These include legs, tails, claws, skin, fur, feathers, scales, beaks, and shells. Animals use these special parts to help them survive in their environments.

Did you know? Every external animal part has a specific job to help the animal stay alive. Some help animals move, others provide protection, and some help animals find food!

Animal Legs: Built for Movement

Animals have different numbers of legs depending on what type of animal they are:

  • Spiders have 8 legs
  • Insects like ants and butterflies have 6 legs
  • Mammals like dogs, cats, cows, lions, and bears have 4 legs
  • Birds have 2 legs
  • Snakes have no legs at all!

A tarantula spider has 8 legs, which help it move, climb, and capture prey. Notice how the legs are covered with tiny hairs that help the spider sense its surroundings.

Animals use their legs to move in different ways - walking, running, jumping, climbing, and digging. A frog's powerful back legs help it jump far distances, while a dog's four legs help it run quickly. Having the right number and type of legs helps animals survive in their habitats.

Claws: Tools for Survival

Many animals have claws at the ends of their toes or fingers. Claws are hard structures that serve multiple purposes:

  • Digging for food (like the armadillo uses its strong claws to dig for insects)
  • Building homes (like rabbits that dig burrows)
  • Climbing trees (like cats and squirrels)
  • Catching prey (like eagles and bears)
  • Defending themselves from danger (like many animals use claws when threatened)

An armadillo uses its powerful claws to dig through soil in search of insects and grubs to eat. These claws are perfectly designed for digging through even hard ground.

The shape and size of an animal's claws tell us a lot about how the animal lives. Animals that climb trees often have curved, sharp claws, while animals that dig have broader, stronger claws.

The armadillo's claws are so powerful that they can break through hard soil and concrete!

Amazing Animal Tails

Tails are another important external part for many animals. Different animals use their tails in different ways:

  • Monkeys use their tails to hang from trees, keeping their hands free to gather food or swing from branch to branch
  • Fish use their tails to swim through water
  • Dogs and cats use their tails for balance when running or walking
  • Lizards have a special ability to detach their tails when caught by predators, allowing them to escape while the predator is distracted by the still-moving tail
  • Horses and cows use their tails to swat away flies and insects

This monkey is using its strong, flexible tail to hang from a tree branch. Its hands remain free to gather food or help it move from branch to branch. This type of tail is called a prehensile tail.

Tails come in many shapes and sizes depending on what job they need to do for the animal. Some are long and thin, others are short and bushy, and some are flat like a beaver's tail.

Animal Coverings: Protection for Every Environment

Animals have different types of coverings on their bodies that serve as protection:

  • Fur - Mammals like polar bears have thick fur that keeps them warm in cold environments
  • Feathers - Birds have feathers that help them fly, stay warm, and sometimes even stay dry in the rain
  • Scales - Fish and reptiles have scales that protect their bodies and help them move through their environments
  • Shells - Animals like turtles, crabs, and snails have hard shells that protect their soft body parts from predators
  • Skin - Animals like humans, elephants, and frogs have skin with special properties. Frog skin is special because it allows them to breathe through it!

This cockatoo's feathers provide multiple benefits: they help the bird fly, keep it warm, and the bright coloring can help attract mates. The bird's beak is also an important external part used for eating and climbing.

Some animal coverings have special patterns or colors that help them survive. For example, zebras have black and white striped patterns on their fur that help them blend in with other zebras in a herd, making it harder for predators like lions to single one out for an attack.

Amazing fact: Frogs can breathe through their skin! Their skin allows oxygen to pass through directly into their bloodstream.

This crab's hard shell serves as armor to protect its body from predators. Its claws help it defend itself, catch food, and communicate with other crabs. The shell also provides support for the crab's body.

Special External Features

Opposable Thumbs

Humans and other primates (like monkeys and apes) have a special feature called opposable thumbs. These thumbs can touch all the other fingers, making it possible to grip objects tightly. This helps with:

  • Grabbing and holding food
  • Using tools
  • Opening containers
  • Climbing trees
  • Using scissors and other tools

Without opposable thumbs, many everyday tasks would be much harder!

Beaks

Birds have beaks instead of mouths with teeth. The shape of a bird's beak tells us what kind of food it eats:

  • Long, thin beaks help birds like hummingbirds drink nectar from flowers
  • Short, strong beaks help birds like cardinals crack open seeds
  • Hooked beaks help birds of prey like eagles tear meat
  • Flat beaks help ducks filter food from water and mud

How External Parts Help Animals Survive

All these external parts work together to help animals:

  • Find and eat food - Like a bird's beak or an armadillo's claws
  • Move around - Like a fish's tail or a dog's legs
  • Stay safe from predators - Like a turtle's shell or a lizard's detachable tail
  • Stay comfortable in their environment - Like a polar bear's fur in cold weather
  • Find mates and have babies - Like the colorful feathers of many male birds

When an animal's external parts are well-suited to its environment, we say the animal is adapted to its habitat.

Vocabulary to Know

External Part: Any part of an animal that is on the outside of it.

Structure: Anything made of parts held together in a specific way.

Survive: To stay alive.

Legs: Animal structures that help them move. Many animal legs are long and touch the ground.

Claws: Hard structures attached to the fingers and toes of some animals that help them dig, scratch, and defend themselves.

Tail: An animal structure that is usually long and attached to the backmost part of an animal.

Camouflage: Colors or patterns that help an animal blend in with its surroundings.

Adaptation: A feature that helps an animal survive in its environment.

Prehensile: Able to grasp or hold objects, like a monkey's tail that can wrap around branches.

Shell: A hard protective covering on the outside of an animal's body, like on a crab or turtle.

  • Tails help with movement, balance, and sometimes even escape from predators
  • Animal coverings like fur, feathers, scales, and shells protect animals in different ways
  • Zebras have striped patterns that help them blend in with the herd to avoid predators
  • Frogs can breathe through their skin, which is a special adaptation
  • Opposable thumbs help humans and primates grip objects
  • Crabs and turtles have hard shells to protect them from predators
